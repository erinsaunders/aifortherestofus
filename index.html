About

I am an HR professional who learned AI. Not the other way around.

Here is what I can promise you: by the end of our four sessions, you will walk away with practical AI skills tailored to your specific role, the confidence to use them, and the professional judgment to know when to trust the output and when to push back. You will not just be more comfortable with AI. You will be using it in ways that make your work faster, sharper, and more distinctly yours.

I am an HR professional with about 20 years of experience, most of it embedded in sales organizations. I am not a tech person who crossed over into HR. I went the other direction. Then, in 2022, I started taking AI seriously.

I did not just open a chatbot and start experimenting. I treated it like any other professional skill worth developing. I followed practitioners like Andrej Karpathy and Nate B. Jones, listened regularly to Beyond the Prompt, read The AI-Driven Leader, and used AI itself to build structured learning curriculums for areas I wanted to go deeper on. And I practiced every day, in real work, with real stakes.

What I found changed how I think about AI entirely. The tool matters less than the judgment you bring to it. AI can draft, synthesize, organize, and accelerate. But it cannot tell you when the output misses the room. It cannot weigh the context that only someone with your experience would know to factor in. That part is yours. The more professional experience you have, the better positioned you are to use AI well, because you already know what good looks like. That is exactly what we build on together.

What's Included

Four sessions designed around how adults actually learn.

Get introduced to something, try it in your real work, come back with more questions, and keep building.

01
Intake  ·  60 minutes

We start by getting to know your role, your workflow, and where you are already bumping up against AI or avoiding it. No homework, no prep required. Just a conversation. From this session, we build your personal roadmap.

We cover the practical stuff: how to set up your tools, how to protect sensitive information, how to prompt effectively. Between sessions you practice. You bring back what worked, what did not, and what surprised you. We go further on your roadmap and address what came up in the real world.

By this session you have a working practice. We sharpen what is left, close any gaps, and you leave with a curated resource list of the Substacks, podcasts, and tools I actually follow to stay current. AI is changing fast. These will help you keep up long after we are done.

FAQ

Got questions?

I'm pretty new to AI. Is this too advanced for me? +
Not at all. This coaching is designed specifically for professionals who are at the beginning of their AI journey or who have dabbled but never felt like they really got traction. We start where you are, not where I assume you should be.
I already use AI a little. Will I get anything out of this? +
Almost certainly yes. Most professionals who are using AI are only scratching the surface of what it can do for their specific work. We will look at your actual workflow, find the gaps, and get practical about how to close them.
What tools do I need? Do I have to buy anything? +
You need access to an AI tool like ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, or similar. Many of these have free tiers that work just fine for our sessions. If you are unsure what you have access to, we can sort that out in our first conversation.
How do we meet? Is this virtual? +
Yes, all sessions are conducted virtually, so you can join from wherever you work.
What if four sessions is not enough? +
Additional sessions are available at $110 each. Some people finish four sessions and feel set. Others want to keep building. Either is fine.
I work in finance, sales, or product management, not HR. Is this relevant to me? +
Absolutely. The skills we cover apply across any professional role. Your industry expertise is actually your biggest asset here. We build on what you already know.
I'm not technical. Am I going to feel dumb in these sessions? +
No. And I say that with some authority because I am not technical either. I am an HR professional who learned to use AI well, which means I learned it the same way you will. There is no jargon, no assumption that you know things you do not, and no judgment. Just practical skills you can use immediately.
